## Key Design Tensions

### 1. Simplicity vs. Distribution Coverage

**Tension:** The simplest distribution is a single `CLAUDE.md` file. Supporting plugin + marketplace + `skills.sh` adds complexity.

**Resolution:** Both paths are maintained in parallel. The `CLAUDE.md` remains the zero-dependency fallback; the plugin path serves users who want cross-project installation without manual file copying. 🟢

**Evidence:** `README.md` documents both install paths with equal prominence.

---

### 2. Normative Source of Truth

**Tension:** With three copies of the rules (`SKILL.md`, `CLAUDE.md`, `SKILL.zh.md`), any edit to the English source must be propagated manually.

**Resolution:** `SKILL.md` is the normative source. `CLAUDE.md` and translation files are derived. No automated sync exists. 🟡 INFERIDO — policy stated implicitly, never documented formally.

**Gap:** See `questions.md`, Q-04.

---

### 3. Community Contributions vs. Maintainer Control

**Tension:** Plugin support, `skills.sh` compatibility, and Chinese localization were all contributed by community members, not the original author. This accelerated distribution coverage but introduced maintenance surface the original author did not design.

**Resolution:** Contributions were merged as-is with no stated ownership transfer or maintenance commitment. 🟢 CONFIRMADO — commit history in `domain.md`.

**Gap:** No CODEOWNERS file; no documented review policy.

## Tradeoff Note (from SKILL.md preamble)

> "These guidelines bias toward caution over speed. For trivial tasks, use judgment."

This meta-rule is a first-class design decision: the guidelines are not applied mechanically. The LLM is trusted to recognize trivial tasks and skip the full rigor when appropriate. The boundary of "trivial" is intentionally left undefined. 🟢 CONFIRMADO — see also `questions.md`, Q-01.
